I'm building a macOS executive intelligence system called Timed that observes a C-suite executive's digital behaviour and builds a compounding cognitive model of them over months. It never acts on the world — observation and intelligence only.

I need a complete implementation spec for estimating the executive's current cognitive load from digital signals — without wearables, without physiological sensors, purely from what the system can observe on their Mac and through their Microsoft 365 environment.

Architecture context: four decoupled layers — Signal Ingestion, Memory Store, Reflection Engine, Intelligence Delivery. Tech: Swift 5.9+, SwiftUI, CoreData, Supabase, Jina embeddings 1024-dim, Claude Haiku/Sonnet/Opus tiered. Nightly Opus reflection, no cost cap. Intelligence compounds.

Research and spec every dimension:

1. **Digital signals as cognitive load proxies** — Research every signal that correlates with cognitive load from digital behaviour:
   - **Email open-to-response latency** — Does increased latency indicate higher load, or disengagement? How to distinguish? Baseline calibration per person.
   - **Draft deletion/revision frequency** — More cognitive load → more revision? Or less load → more perfectionism? What does the research say?
   - **App-switch rate** — Rapid context switching as a load indicator. Gonzalez & Mark's research on multitasking and attention fragmentation. What switch rate thresholds indicate overload for an individual?
   - **Meeting density** — Consecutive meetings with no buffer. Research on meeting load and decision quality degradation. Luong & Rogelberg on meeting recovery time.
   - **Calendar whitespace usage** — When the executive has a free hour, do they immediately fill it? Whitespace consumption rate as a load signal.
   - **Typing speed and error rate** — Keystroke dynamics as cognitive load proxy. Vizer et al. research. Implementation on macOS via accessibility APIs without keylogging content.
   - **Communication brevity shift** — Emails getting shorter. Responses becoming terse. Reduced "warmth" tokens (greetings, sign-offs). Load-driven communication compression.
   - **Decision deferral rate** — How many decisions are being pushed to "later"? Increased deferral as a load signal.
   - **Time-to-first-action after meetings** — How long before the executive does anything productive after a meeting ends? Longer latency = higher residual load.
   - **Browser/app tab accumulation** — Open tab count as an unfinished-task proxy (Zeigarnik effect in digital form).
   
   For each signal: research supporting it as a load proxy, measurement approach on macOS, noise characteristics, individual calibration requirements.

2. **Composite load model** — How to combine 8-12 noisy proxy signals into a single cognitive load estimate. Approaches:
   - Weighted linear combination (simple, interpretable, fragile)
   - Bayesian sensor fusion (robust, handles missing signals)
   - Small neural network trained on individual data (requires labelled data — where does it come from?)
   - LLM-based reasoning over signal vector (expensive but can incorporate context)
   Which approach is best for a single-user system with limited initial training data? How to bootstrap?

3. **Real-time vs trailing estimate** — Should the system produce:
   - A real-time load estimate (updated every 5-15 minutes)?
   - A trailing estimate (rolling 2-hour average)?
   - Both, at different granularities?
   What's the latency requirement for load estimation to be useful for gating intelligence delivery? Research on cognitive load temporal dynamics — how fast does load change?

4. **Load-aware intelligence gating** — The primary use of the cognitive load model: don't deliver strategic insights or complex intelligence when the executive is overloaded. Spec the gating logic:
   - Load levels: {low, moderate, high, critical}
   - Per load level: what intelligence is appropriate to surface?

   - Moderate: essential items only, compressed format
   - High: urgent-only, one-line alerts
   - Critical: silence unless emergency
   How to avoid the trap where the system never delivers intelligence because the executive is always loaded?

5. **Individual calibration** — Every person's baseline is different. A CEO in a turnaround has a permanently higher load baseline than one in steady state. How to calibrate:
   - Personal baseline establishment (first 2 weeks)
   - Relative vs absolute load measurement
   - Baseline drift detection (new normal vs temporary spike)
   - Day-of-week baseline variation (Monday vs Friday)

Primary drivers: 3 additional board-related meetings and the product launch." Load trends as input to longer-term pattern analysis.
