# I am building Timed a macOS application designed as the most intelligent executive operating system ever built. Timed passively observes C-suite executives through email metadata via Microsoft Graph API, calendar structure, keystroke dynamics, on-device voice analysis, and application usage patterns. It never takes any action on the world it never sends emails, never modifies calendars, never does anything. The human always decides and executes. Timed builds a compounding cognitive model of the executive that gets qualitatively smarter every month. It is built in Swift/macOS with a Supabase backend, Microsoft Graph API for email and calendar integration, and Claude API (Haiku/Sonnet/Opus 4.6) for intelligence processing. Critical constraint there is no cost cap on this system the only metric that matters is intelligence quality. Never suggest cheaper alternatives or cost optimisations.

My research question is How do you make a personal cognitive intelligence system genuinely valuable from day 1, before it has learned anything specific about this particular executive?

The cold start problem is existential for Timed. If the system is not valuable immediately, the executive will not give it the weeks and months it needs to build a truly personalised cognitive model. I need deep, comprehensive answers to the following six sub-questions.

Sub-question 1 What Frontier Models Can Infer from 48 Hours of Observation

What can a frontier language model like Claude Opus 4.6, with deep contextual prompting, infer about an executive from just the first 48 hours of email metadata, calendar structure, and application usage using base-rate intelligence about C-suite cognition as a prior? I need the specific research on thin-slice judgments in psychology Ambady, Rosenthal, first-impression accuracy and its computational replicability, what demographic and organisational features can be reliably inferred from email and calendar structure alone Kosinskis work on digital footprint inference, Eagles work on communication network analysis. What are the specific inferences that are reliably accurate from minimal data versus those that require extended observation? For example can you reliably infer an executives decision-making style deliberative vs intuitive from their first 48 hours of calendar patterns and email response time distributions? What about their communication style, their delegation patterns, their meeting preferences? I need the boundary between what is achievable with high confidence from thin-slice data and what requires weeks or months of observation.

Sub-question 2 Optimal Onboarding Sequence Design

What is the optimal onboarding sequence structured interview, initial questions, self-reported data that extracts the highest-value seed information with the minimum friction for a time-starved C-suite executive? I need the research on intake interview design in executive coaching what questions do the best executive coaches ask first?, structured interviews in clinical psychology that maximise information extraction in minimal time, the specific self-report instruments that reliably capture decision-making style, risk tolerance, cognitive style REI Rational-Experiential Inventory, MBTI limitations and alternatives, Big Five and its predictive validity for executive behaviour. How long should the onboarding take 5 minutes, 15 minutes, 45 minutes? What is the diminishing return curve? What information should be asked versus inferred and when is asking actually lower quality than inferring because executives have inaccurate self-models? Include research on self-report accuracy for cognitive and behavioural traits Vazires self-other knowledge asymmetry model what do people know about themselves that others dont, and vice versa. Design the specific sequence what is asked first, what is asked second, what is deferred until the system has observational data to compare against self-report.

Sub-question 3 Rapid Rapport and First-Impression Accuracy

What does the research on rapid rapport building and first-impression accuracy tell us that can be replicated computationally? I need the specific research on the accuracy of first impressions from behavioural cues Ambadys thin-slice research applied to professional contexts, the role of mirroring and adaptation in rapport formation can a text-based system mirror communication style from the first interaction?, the specific elements of an AI systems first communication that build versus destroy trust. How do the best executive coaches establish credibility in the first meeting and what elements of that are replicable in an AI system? Include research on the Pygmalion effect and expectation management does telling an executive the system will become dramatically more intelligent over time increase or decrease initial engagement? What is the optimal tone, formality level, and specificity for the systems first communications with a Fortune 500 CEO too casual undermines credibility, too formal feels generic. What does research on physician bedside manner, therapist alliance formation, and executive coach rapport tell us about the computational replication of trust-building?

Sub-question 4 Default Intelligence Universally Valuable for Any C-Suite Executive

What intelligence is universally relevant to C-suite executives and can be delivered before any personalisation? I need the specific research on what are the universal patterns of C-suite time allocation Porter and Nohrias CEO time study specific findings on meeting patterns, alone time, communication modalities, what decision-making frameworks are universally applicable Kleins Recognition-Primed Decision model, Kahnemans System 1/System 2 applied to executive decisions, Eisenhardts fast strategic decision-making, what calendar and email patterns universally predict problems McKinsey research on organisational health indicators visible in communication patterns. What specific insights can the system deliver on day 1 that are valuable to any executive Based on your calendar, you have 3 hours of fragmented time this week that research suggests cannot be used for deep work or Your email response time distribution suggests you are processing email reactively rather than in batches here is what the research says about the cognitive cost. Design the full library of default intelligence the 20-30 specific insights that can be generated from raw calendar and email structure using only base-rate executive cognition research, before any personalisation.

Sub-question 5 Managing the Expectation Gap

How do you manage the gap between generic and personalised intelligence so the executive stays engaged during the learning period? I need the research on expectation management in technology adoption, the trough of disillusionment in individual product adoption not just Gartners macro curve, but individual-level adoption curves, how to frame improving performance over time as a feature rather than a limitation. What does the research on AI system transparency say about communicating learning progress should the system say I am 15% through learning your patterns or is quantification harmful? How do subscription-based services with improving quality over time Spotify Discover Weekly, Netflix recommendations manage the expectation gap, and what translates to a professional intelligence context versus what is consumer-specific? Include research on the endowed progress effect Nunes and Dreze and how artificial advancement can increase engagement. What is the specific risk of the executive concluding this is just generic advice and uninstalling before the system crosses the personalisation threshold and how is this risk mitigated through design, communication, and early value delivery?

Sub-question 6 Minimum Observation Period and Value Trajectory

What is the minimum observation period before the first genuinely personalised insight, and what does the value trajectory look like from day 1 to week 4 to month 3? I need the research on how long behavioural pattern recognition takes for different signal types email patterns stabilise faster than decision-making patterns, the concept of learning curves in personalisation systems collaborative filtering warm-up times, individual model convergence rates, and what the value delivery schedule should look like. Design the specific value trajectory what intelligence is delivered on day 1 base-rate, day 3 first thin-slice inferences, week 1 first pattern observations, week 2 first cross-signal correlations, week 4 first predictive insights, month 3 first deep cognitive model outputs. For each milestone, what is the confidence level, the specificity of the insight, and the executives expected reaction? Include research on how long it takes executive coaches to build accurate mental models of their clients typical engagement timelines, when coaches report feeling they understand their client, as a human baseline for the computational system to match or exceed.

Required output format 1. Onboarding sequence design a day-by-day plan for week 1, with specific questions, data collection, and value delivery at each step 2. Cold start intelligence architecture the specific pipeline from raw data + base-rate knowledge to day-1 insights, including prompt engineering patterns for Claude Opus 4.6 3. Default intelligence library the 20-30 specific base-rate insights that can be delivered to any executive from calendar/email structure alone 4. Value trajectory framework the specific milestones from day 1 to month 3, with what intelligence is delivered at each point, expected confidence levels, and the executives expected experience

For every claim, cite the specific research. For every design recommendation, explain why it is optimal for a time-starved Fortune 500 CEO. Prioritise depth over breadth I need implementable specificity, not survey overviews.

Here is the full deep-dive report on Timed's cold start architecture. It covers all six sub-questions with full research citations and all four required deliverables.

**Key architectural findings:**

The cold start problem is fundamentally a **credibility problem, not a data problem.** Claude Opus 4.6 can generate legitimate day-1 value precisely because it operates from a well-calibrated prior over C-suite behavior — individual data is a correction term, not the ground truth.

The six sub-questions produced several non-obvious design decisions:

1. **Thin-slice inference is trait-dependent.** Extraversion, network centrality, and communication energy are reliably inferable from 48 hours. Conscientiousness, decision-making style, and agreeableness require 2–4 weeks minimum. The 578-executive email study produced 83.56% top-performer identification accuracy from metadata alone — this is the direct evidence base for Timed's email pipeline.[^1_1]
2. **The SOKA model inverts the intuitive onboarding design.** Ask about neuroticism/internal experience (only the executive knows); infer intellect, leadership effectiveness, and communication style behaviorally (Vazire's research shows others outperform self-report for evaluative traits). Never ask an executive how reactive they are — their calendar tells a more honest story.[^1_2][^1_3]
3. **12-minute onboarding is the optimal ceiling.** Beyond that point, the marginal information gain does not justify the attention cost for a time-starved C-suite executive, and behavioral data will correct self-reports within days anyway.
4. **The endowed progress effect is the bridge across the individual trough of disillusionment.** Pre-populating the cognitive model with dimensions that are immediately measurable (communication style: observable from hour 1) versus explicitly leaving others empty (decision-making style: requires 3–4 weeks) — with reasons provided for each — produces the 34% vs. 19% completion rate difference Nunes and Drèze documented.[^1_4][^1_5]
5. **Quantified learning progress destroys trust; qualitative calibration builds it.** "I'm 15% through learning your patterns" is worse than "I can now distinguish your strategic contacts from your operational contacts but I don't yet have enough data to comment on how your communication style shifts under board-level pressure."
