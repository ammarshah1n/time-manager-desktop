# Relationship Intelligence Card Design for C-Suite Executive OS

**Build Brief for Claude Opus 4.6 — Timed-Brain RelationshipHealthService**

***

## Executive Summary

This brief specifies the complete information architecture, visual format, content structure, and ethical framework for relationship intelligence cards generated by the Timed-Brain executive OS. The system passively monitors email and calendar signals for 15–20 key contacts, generates cards when health scores deteriorate, and presents time-constrained C-suite users with actionable interpersonal intelligence. The card design is grounded in validated cognitive load theory, organizational communication research, enterprise relationship health tooling, and Apple's Human Interface Guidelines for macOS SwiftUI.

Key design constraints from the evidence:
- Working memory capacity: ~4 meaningful chunks (Cowan 2001); executive glance time: 15–30 seconds[^1]
- Response latency is the single most empirically validated signal of relationship connection (PNAS 2022)
- Email tone can be automatically scored for frustration (ICC=0.506), formality (ICC=0.73), and politeness (ICC=0.64) using psycholinguistic features (ACL 2018)[see URL fetch above]
- Uncertainty must be surfaced explicitly; AI without uncertainty cues drives 54% overtrust vs 28% when uncertainty is communicated (Vaccaro et al., MIT 2023)[^2]
- Self-monitoring of one's own email/calendar metadata sits under GDPR Article 6(1)(f) legitimate interests; third-party signal analysis requires careful scoping under UK ICO guidance[^3][^4]

## SQ1 — Existing Enterprise Relationship Intelligence Tools

### Affinity CRM

Affinity's patented relationship intelligence engine ingests every email, meeting, and interaction to derive a relationship strength score based on recency and frequency weighting. The platform automatically captures "data exhaust" — names, roles, industries, source of introduction — eliminating manual entry. The UI surfaces relationship strength as a continuous score between two parties derived from historical interaction data, with a separate decay algorithm that deprioritises stale interactions. Affinity flags contacts below a nurture threshold and can trigger outreach recommendations when scores drop. Key signal fields include: interaction recency, interaction frequency, initiation direction (who reached out first), connection paths (warm introduction routes), and third-party enrichment data.[^5]

### Gong Engage Analytics

Gong's analytics layer surfaces the following tracked fields at the contact level:[^6][^7]

- **Emails sent** — total outbound to external recipients (excluding auto-replies and calendar invites)
- **People replied** — distinct external recipients who replied within date range
- **Reply rate** — replies ÷ emails sent
- **Meetings booked** — calendar events created with externals, excluding recurring
- **Call connect rate** — answered calls / total dialer calls
- **Avg. call duration** — mean minutes of recorded connected calls
- **Interactivity score** — conversation direction switches per call; Gong's validated benchmark is ≥5 switches for productive calls[^7]
- **Email outcomes (AI-generated)** — categorised as: Wants to meet / Wants info / Interested / Referral (positive); Has a solution (neutral); Bad timing / Bad persona / Not interested / Opt out (negative)[^8]
- **Talk-time ratio** — Gong identifies 43% prospect / 57% rep as the optimal ratio; deviations are flagged[^9]

Gong also performs sentiment and emotion analysis on calls, identifying positive, neutral, and negative indicators throughout interactions.[^9]

### People.ai

People.ai's Data Foundation aggregates inbox data, auto-generates contact records, applies title taxonomy, and maps roles/hierarchy/influence within accounts. The platform flags weak engagement, missing contacts, and stalling momentum by comparing current activity against expected baselines. The "most engaged contacts" — as indicated by People.ai's proprietary engagement score — are surfaced as suggested matches in opportunity scorecards. The engagement score is derived from the volume and recency of email and meeting activity mapped to open opportunities.[^10][^11]

Staircase AI (now Gainsight's Insight Agent) provides the most granular sub-score architecture of any commercial tool reviewed:[^12][^13]

| Sub-Score | Description | Scale | Method |
|---|---|---|---|
| Sentiment | Tone analysis of emails, chats, tickets | 0–100 | NPS-style model + time-decay weighting[^12] |
| Engagement | Interaction frequency and depth | 0–100 | Rolling window, configurable look-back days[^12] |
| Response Time | Bidirectional latency both parties | 0–100 | Relative score vs. account baseline[^12] |
| Open Items | Unresolved support issues/actions pending | 0–100 | Ticket + task count[^13] |

Gainsight recommends 4–6 key metrics total for health scoring, with usage at 40%, support trends at 25%, sentiment at 20%, and executive engagement at 15% as example weightings. The SMB-tier recommendation reduces engagement weight and redistributes to sentiment, open items, and response time when engagement volume is structurally low.[^14][^12]

### LinkedIn Sales Navigator Relationship Map

Sales Navigator's Relationship Map renders a drag-and-drop hierarchical view of contacts with five predefined roles: Decision Maker, Champion, Evaluator, Procurement, Influencer. The list view includes per-contact notes, relationship strength scoring, and a scoring system for tracking relationship quality over time. Recent events (job changes, news mentions) are highlighted as re-engagement triggers. Account IQ (launched 2023) uses generative AI to synthesise account-level summaries blending LinkedIn first-party data (posts, workforce trends) with third-party sources.[^15][^16]

**Critical gap across all tools reviewed:** None of the above tools specifically surface *multi-interpretation uncertainty* for latency spikes. They display scores without flagging that a given data pattern has multiple plausible causes. This represents a design gap the Timed-Brain card must fill.

## SQ2 — Executive Intelligence Consumption Preferences

### The Intelligence Community Precedent

The CIA's Presidential Daily Brief (PDB) format is the most rigorously battle-tested model for delivering interpersonal and geopolitical intelligence to time-constrained decision-makers. PDB design principles include: headline-first construction, 3–5 bullet points per item, no paragraph prose, and tight calibration to the receiver's specific operational priorities. Each item on a PDB page is a self-contained intelligence card — the receiver should be able to process the item, understand its implication, and make a decision in under 30 seconds.[^17]

The Pyramid Principle (Minto, 1987), widely adopted in top-tier management consulting, extends this: conclusions precede supporting evidence, not the reverse. Executives operating under cognitive load respond to structure that leads with the "so what" and subordinates data to justify it. Dense visual presentation — unlike what might work for analysts — signals unclear thinking to executive audiences and reduces trust in the underlying intelligence.[^18]

### Narrative vs. Metric Format

Narrative intelligence tools work well for team dynamics and extended coaching contexts, where stakeholders have time to ingest stories and extract patterns. For time-compressed C-suite contexts, the literature supports a **metric-anchored, narrative-framed hybrid**: a quantitative signal triggers the card, a concise natural-language synthesis frames its meaning, and narrative context is available on expand. Goleman's executive coaching research identifies that few organisations have explicit metrics on the emotional climate a leader creates — making the card's interpretive layer particularly high-value.[^19][^20]

Executive coaching engagements that generate the most behaviour change focus on 1–3 measurable metrics with pre/post tracking, rather than exhaustive dashboards. Translating this to card design: **surface 1–3 salient signals per card, not all available data**.[^21]

### The "Signalling" vs. "Informing" Distinction

Academy of Management research (Journals AOM, 2021) establishes that email reply priorities are governed by four heuristics: arrival order, inferrable subject-line content, reply effort, and sender timing expectations. Crucially, emails from superiors are not responded to faster based on authority alone — timing expectations are the dominant variable. This implies that a CEO's *own* reply latency baseline carries meaning: when the CEO deviates from their own historical pattern (replying later than their norm), it signals a structural shift in attention allocation, not merely busyness.[^22]

Violations of organisational timing norms — out-of-sync replies — predict employee turnover and create communication bottlenecks. This finding directly validates tracking timing-norm deviation (delta from personal baseline) as a primary card-triggering metric.[^22]

## SQ3 — Metrics Evaluation: Predictive Power and Signal Validity

### Response Latency

**Strongest empirically validated signal.** Templeton et al. (PNAS 2022) across three studies with 66 participants and 450 MTurk judges demonstrates that faster response times are a *robust and sufficient* signal of social connection, with effect sizes: enjoyment b=−0.35 (p<0.001), connection b=−0.28 (p<0.001) across conversations; partner responsivity drives the effect more strongly than self-responsivity (partner b=−0.27 vs. self b=−0.11). Critically, response times under 250ms preclude conscious control, making them an *honest signal* that cannot be strategically faked. For email, this converts to: a contact's median reply latency to the CEO's emails is a bidirectional relationship health indicator.

AOM 2021 establishes that timing-norm violations — consistent out-of-sync replies — are a predictor of organisational disengagement and turnover. The card should track **delta from personal baseline** (each contact's own historical reply norm), not absolute latency.[^23][^22]

Cornell research (2021) identifies that senders and receivers systematically have divergent response-speed expectations — receivers assume senders expect faster replies than they actually do (email urgency bias). The card should correct for this bias by anchoring latency interpretation to the contact's personal baseline, not a universal norm. Industry norms (24–48 hours for general professional email) serve as a floor for anomaly detection, not the primary benchmark.[^24][^25][^26]

### Email Tone / Sentiment

Chhaya et al. (ACL 2018) provide the foundational computational model for frustration, formality, and politeness detection in email using the Enron corpus (960 emails). Key validated findings[see URL fetch above]:
- **Frustration prediction**: Ridge regression with lexical + syntactic + affect features achieves MSE=0.0212; Random Forest classifier achieves 86% accuracy (precision=0.88, recall=0.85, F1=0.85)
- **Formality prediction**: ICC=0.73 inter-annotator agreement; longer emails are more formal
- **Politeness prediction**: ICC=0.64 inter-annotator agreement
- **Key finding**: Frustration, formality, and politeness are *negatively correlated* — high frustration co-occurs with low formality and low politeness. A drop in formality index across a thread is a compound frustration signal[see URL fetch above]
- Feature importance: PERMA positivity lexicon features, Emolex-Intensity-Sadness, and ANEW-arousal are the most predictive affect features for frustration[see URL fetch above]

Practical implication: The tone index computed per email thread should track the Formality + Politeness composite (high values = healthy) and the Frustration sub-score (low values = healthy). A downward shift in Formality/Politeness or upward shift in Frustration across the trailing 30-day window is a validated deterioration signal.

### Meeting Attendance / Cancellation Rate

Meeting attendance behaviour carries strong relationship signal. Research on meeting behaviours (DigitalCommons@UNO) finds that counterproductive meeting behaviours reduce employee engagement and increase emotional exhaustion. The Gainsight/Staircase model includes meeting frequency in its Engagement sub-score. Cancellation is a stronger signal than non-attendance because it is a deliberate act, not a scheduling conflict.[^12]

The card should distinguish three states: **Attended**, **Declined (with notice)**, **Cancelled (after acceptance)**. Cancelled-after-acceptance is the highest-signal deterioration indicator because it represents withdrawn commitment, not pre-emptive priority management.

### Initiated vs. Received Ratio

ONA research consistently shows that high-productivity individuals message *more* people and respond to more contacts — they are communication bridges. The initiated-vs-received ratio therefore captures directionality of investment. An executive who has historically initiated 60% of communications with a contact, dropping to 20%, signals cooling interest on the executive's side. A contact who has historically initiated 50% dropping to 10% signals cooling on their side. Tracking ratio trend (trailing 90 days vs. prior 90 days) surfaces which party is withdrawing.[^27]

### Topic Overlap / Divergence

LDA and biterm models have been applied to email corpora to identify topic clusters and drift. Topic divergence — where the subject matter of email threads shifts from strategic/high-value topics (board matters, investment, partnership) to administrative/low-value topics (scheduling, logistics) — is a leading indicator of relationship deprioritisation. This requires tracking the topic cluster distribution per contact over time. High semantic overlap between current and prior-period emails indicates stable relationship focus; divergence indicates either growth (new topics = expanded relationship scope) or decay (from strategic to transactional).[^28]

**Gap acknowledgment**: No peer-reviewed study directly validates topic divergence as a standalone predictor of professional relationship deterioration. The signal has face validity from NLP literature and ONA theory but has not been isolated as a causal predictor. Treat as a secondary signal requiring corroboration.

### Availability/Responsiveness

Calendar acceptance rate — the proportion of meeting invites accepted within 48 hours — is a validated proxy for relationship priority. The Affinity decay algorithm implicitly captures this via interaction recency. Staircase AI's Response Time sub-score explicitly models bidirectional responsiveness on a 0–100 scale. The card should surface calendar acceptance rate as a secondary confirmation metric when latency signals are ambiguous.[^12]

### Decision Matrix

| Metric | Validated Predictive Power | Privacy Risk Level | Executive Preference Rank |
|---|---|---|---|
| Reply latency delta (from personal baseline) | **High** — PNAS 2022 (p<0.001); AOM 2021 turnover predictor[^22] | Low — metadata, no content | 1 |
| Meeting cancellation rate (post-acceptance) | **High** — strongest behavioural commitment signal; face validity; no direct peer-review isolation | Low — calendar metadata | 2 |
| Tone/formality index (trailing 30-day) | **Moderate-High** — ACL 2018 ICC=0.73 formality, 0.64 politeness[see URL fetch] | Medium — requires content access | 3 |
| Email frequency trend (contact-initiated) | **Moderate** — ONA productivity correlates; Affinity decay model[^27][^5] | Low — header metadata | 4 |
| Initiated vs. received ratio | **Moderate** — ONA theory support; no isolated peer-review validation | Low — metadata | 5 |
| Calendar acceptance latency | **Moderate** — implied by Staircase RT sub-score[^12] | Low — metadata | 6 |
| Topic overlap / divergence | **Low-Moderate** — LDA/biterm support[^28]; no direct causal study | High — requires deep content parsing | 7 |
| Frustration sub-score | **Moderate** — ACL 2018 RF 86% accuracy[see URL fetch] | High — requires content access | 8 |

## SQ4 — Uncertainty Framing and Epistemic Integrity

The failure mode for relationship intelligence systems is false confidence: a latency spike is labelled "disengagement" when it could equally be travel, strategic silence, overload, or a deliberate communication style shift. This destroys executive trust in the system rapidly.

### The BODHI Framework (Medical AI, adapted)

The BODHI framework (PMC 2026) for high-stakes AI systems specifies: *precision when confident, transparency about limitations when uncertain*. It operationalises humility through four dimensions: open-mindedness, intellectual modesty, corrigibility, and active engagement. Applied to relationship intelligence:[^29]

- **Open-mindedness**: The card must present multiple interpretations, not a single conclusion
- **Intellectual modesty**: Confidence levels (high/medium/low) must accompany each interpretation
- **Corrigibility**: The CEO must be able to dismiss or reframe any interpretation, and the system must learn from that feedback
- **Active engagement**: When uncertainty is high, the card should prompt for context (e.g., "Do you know if [contact] is travelling this week?") rather than auto-concluding

MIT 2023 (Vaccaro et al.) quantifies the cost of skipping uncertainty cues: participants accepted incorrect AI recommendations 54% of the time when no uncertainty was communicated, vs. 28% when uncertainty was surfaced. The implication for a CEO reading 15–20 relationship cards: skipping uncertainty framing will cause systematic misreading of relationship health at scale.[^2]

### Multi-Interpretation Protocol

For every trigger event, Claude Opus should generate **2–4 competing interpretations ranked by prior probability**, not a single diagnosis. The card template:

```
SIGNAL: Reply latency increased 4.2× vs. 30-day baseline
MOST LIKELY: Heavy workload / travel (67% — consistent with increased email volume from their end)
POSSIBLE: Deliberate deprioritisation (21% — no explanation provided for 2 missed meeting requests)
POSSIBLE: External disruption (8% — LinkedIn shows role change 3 weeks ago)
UNLIKELY: Relationship disengagement (4% — tone metrics stable, last meeting attended)
```

Probability estimates should be derived from available corroborating signals, not from priors alone. When corroborating signals conflict (e.g., tone is stable but frequency is down), the card should label the situation explicitly: **"Mixed signals — insufficient data for confident interpretation."**

### Calibration Anchors

The arXiv 2026 total uncertainty framework separates epistemic uncertainty (model uncertainty) from aleatoric uncertainty (inherent data variability). The card system should propagate both:[^30]
- **Epistemic uncertainty**: flagged when the model has insufficient history (< 30 days of baseline data, or a contact added within the last 14 days)
- **Aleatoric uncertainty**: flagged when the signal itself is inherently noisy (e.g., tone scores based on < 3 emails in the analysis window)

When either uncertainty type exceeds threshold, the card should display a reduced-confidence banner rather than normal health status indicators.

## SQ5 — Privacy, Ethics, and Legal Guardrails

### GDPR and UK GDPR Legal Basis

Under UK GDPR Article 6, processing must rest on one of six lawful bases. The self-monitoring use case — where the CEO monitors their *own* email/calendar metadata to derive signals about their relationship patterns — is most defensible under **legitimate interests** (Article 6(1)(f)), as the processing serves the controller's direct operational interest and does not require accessing counterparty data without justification.[^31][^3]

The UK ICO's updated 2026 workplace monitoring guidance specifies that employers must: select the least intrusive monitoring means, monitor in ways workers would reasonably expect, avoid function creep, and complete DPIAs for high-risk monitoring activities. This applies to employee monitoring, but the ICO's anti-function-creep principle applies by analogy to any automated relationship scoring system: the system must not use data collected for one purpose (email management) to derive inferences for another purpose (counterparty behavioural profiling) without explicit scoping.[^4]

**H&M precedent**: The €35.3M fine for monitoring employees without knowledge or consent illustrates the enforcement appetite for undisclosed interpersonal data processing. The card system does not process employees' data in the traditional employment sense, but the principle of minimal footprint applies.

**Practical legal architecture for Timed-Brain:**
- All analysis runs locally on-device (no third-party cloud transmission of email content)
- Tone analysis uses NLP models operating on user-device encrypted storage
- The system processes the CEO's own sent/received metadata; counterparty content is processed only as received in the CEO's own inbox (no external data enrichment from third-party sources without explicit opt-in)
- Relationship intelligence is retained in the Supabase instance under the CEO's control, with configurable retention windows and deletion on contact removal

### Ethical Guardrails

The card must surface the following ethical constraints explicitly to the user at onboarding and in the card UI:

1. **Interpretive limits disclosure**: "This analysis is based on communication patterns, not intent. Signals have multiple valid explanations."
2. **No autonomous action**: The system observes and reflects. It never drafts emails, sends communications, or modifies calendar entries without explicit CEO instruction.
3. **Contact awareness option**: For internal contacts (direct reports, co-founders), consider whether subjects should be informed that relationship health scoring is in use.
4. **Recency bias correction**: All scores use time-decay weighting to prevent over-indexing on stale data points.
5. **Feedback loop transparency**: When the CEO dismisses a card's interpretation, the system must log the dismissal and recalibrate — not silently discard it.

## SQ6 — Information Density Optimisation

### Working Memory Constraints

Miller (1956) established the "magical number seven" as working memory capacity; Cowan (2001) revised this to approximately four chunks for young adults, with fewer in older adults and under cognitive load. Fixed-capacity visual WM models converge on ~4 discrete slots. For a CEO reading a notification card while multitasking, the effective working memory budget is conservatively **3–4 items per display layer**.[^32][^33][^1]

Dr. Gloria Mark (UC Irvine) documents that attention switches occur every 47 seconds on digital devices, down from 2.5 minutes in 2004. The mean attention window for digital content is under 45 seconds. Combined with the Forgetting Curve (70% of new information forgotten within 24 hours without reinforcement), this constrains the card's effective delivery window.[^34]

### Optimal Reading Time by Layer

| Layer | Target Reading Time | Max Data Points | Retention Goal |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Glance** (notification banner / collapsed card) | 5–8 seconds | 3 items | Contact name, health status, primary signal |
| **Summary** (expanded card, no drill-down) | 15–30 seconds | 6–8 items | Trend summary, top interpretations, recommended action |
| **Detail** (drill-down / sheet view) | 60–90 seconds | Unlimited (scrollable) | Full signal log, metric history, raw data |

Microlearning research from IACET finds that interactivity paired with short content bursts increases retention from 21% to 95%. The card should include a single high-confidence recommended action at the Summary layer — making the card interactive (a decision prompt) rather than purely passive — to maximise retention and executive engagement.[^34]

### Cognitive Load Management

Sweller's Cognitive Load Theory (1988, replicated widely) distinguishes intrinsic load (complexity of the task itself), extraneous load (poor design friction), and germane load (learning/schema-building). Card design should minimise extraneous load through:[^33]
- Consistent visual schema (same layout for every contact's card; no surprises)
- Progressive disclosure (complexity revealed on demand, not by default)
- Chunked grouping (signals grouped by category: Frequency, Tone, Availability)
- One primary action per layer (not a menu of options at each level)

## SQ7 — Native macOS SwiftUI Patterns

### Apple HIG Core Principles Applied

Apple's HIG specifies six foundational principles: Hierarchy (visual priority ordering), Harmony (coordination with platform aesthetics), Consistency (familiar patterns), Clarity (legibility and focus), Deference (UI should serve content, not assert itself), Depth (spatial metaphors for context). The relationship card must exhibit strong Hierarchy (the health delta is the most prominent element) and strong Deference (the card provides context for the CEO's own relationships — it must not feel like an external system imposing judgement).[^35]

**Layout specification**: Apple HIG explicitly states progressive disclosure should "take advantage of progressive disclosure to help people discover content". The disclosure control (`DisclosureGroup` in SwiftUI) provides a collapsed/expanded toggle bound to an `isExpanded: Binding<Bool>` state. Each contact card should be implemented as a `DisclosureGroup` with independent `isExpanded` bindings (not shared state, which causes all groups to expand/collapse simultaneously).[^36][^37][^38][^35]

### SwiftUI Component Architecture

**Glance Layer (collapsed `DisclosureGroup`):**
```swift
DisclosureGroup(isExpanded: $card.isExpanded) {
    // Detail content
} label: {
    RelationshipGlanceRow(
        name: contact.displayName,
        healthDelta: card.healthDelta,
        primarySignal: card.primarySignalDescription,
        statusColor: card.statusColor // .green / .yellow / .red
    )
}
```

**Summary Layer (expanded, pre-drill-down):**
- `VStack` containing: trend sparklines (using Swift Charts), interpretation list (ranked by probability), recommended action button
- Swift Charts (introduced WWDC22) supports inline sparkline rendering suitable for compact trend visualisation within a card

**Detail Layer (sheet presentation):**
- Triggered by "View full analysis" button; presented as `.sheet` on macOS[^39]
- Contains full signal log, 90-day history charts, raw metric values, feedback controls (dismiss/confirm interpretation)

**Popover for contextual tooltips:**
- Apple HIG specifies popovers for "transient view that appears above other content when people click or tap a control"[^40]
- Use `.popover` for metric-definition tooltips (e.g., "What is Reply Latency Delta?")
- macOS-specific: popover attachment anchor position is configurable, unlike iOS where it adapts to compact mode[^41]

**Notification trigger:**
- Apple HIG notification principle: "timely, high-value information people can understand at a glance"[^42]
- Relationship health deterioration notification should use `UNUserNotificationContent` with `categoryIdentifier` for actionable responses (Dismiss / View Card)
- Notification banner should contain: contact name, primary signal in 5–8 words, and health status colour indicator

| 10 | `tone_formality_index` | Float (0–1) | NLP (ACL 2018 model: lexical + syntactic + affect features) | Gauge with 30-day baseline band | Summary |
| 11 | `interpretation_set` | Array[{label, probability, corroborating_signals}] | Claude Opus synthesis | Ranked list with probability bars | Summary |
| 12 | `recommended_action` | String | Claude Opus synthesis | CTA button, ≤8 words | Summary |
| 13 | `confidence_level` | Enum (high/medium/low) | Epistemic + aleatoric uncertainty composite | Confidence badge | Summary |

**Schema notes:**
- Fields 1–5 render in the collapsed `DisclosureGroup` label (Glance layer)
- Fields 6–13 render in the expanded `DisclosureGroup` content (Summary layer)
- Fields 14–15 render in the `.sheet` drill-down (Detail layer)
- `grey` health status is reserved for insufficient data (< 14 days baseline) — prevents false confidence on new contacts
- `confidence_level` is computed as: high if ≥3 corroborating signals agree; medium if 2 agree; low if signals conflict or data window < 30 days

## Progressive Disclosure Hierarchy

### Layer 1 — Glance (5–8 seconds; 3 data points)
Rendered in macOS notification banner and as collapsed `DisclosureGroup` row:
- Contact name (with role/company subtitle)
- Health status colour + delta percentage
- Primary signal in ≤12 words (e.g., "Reply latency up 4.2× vs. your 30-day baseline")

**Decision required at this layer:** "Is this worth reading now?" If yes, tap to expand.

### Layer 2 — Summary (15–30 seconds; 6–8 data points)
Rendered in expanded `DisclosureGroup`:
- Reply latency delta (sparkline + absolute value)
- Meeting attendance rate (% + cancel/decline breakdown)
- Email frequency trend (7-week sparkline)
- Initiated ratio delta (current vs. prior period)
- Interpretation set (2–4 ranked interpretations with probability)
- Confidence level badge
- Recommended action (primary CTA button)

**Decision required at this layer:** "Should I act on the recommended action?" If yes, tap to execute or schedule. If the situation is unclear, tap "View full analysis."

### Layer 3 — Detail (60–90 seconds; unlimited scrollable)
Rendered in `.sheet` presentation:
- Full signal timeline (chronological, tagged by metric category)
- 90-day charts for each tracked metric (Swift Charts)
- Tone index history with thread-level drill-down
- Raw data table (optional — behind secondary disclosure)
- Feedback controls: "Confirm interpretation" / "Dismiss — I know why" / "Add context" (freeform)

**Decision required at this layer:** "Does my contextual knowledge change the interpretation?" The CEO can annotate ("Alex is on sabbatical") which feeds back into the system's interpretation calibration.

## Implementation Notes for RelationshipHealthService Integration

Based on the architecture implied by the Timed-Brain repository:

1. **Trigger logic**: Cards are generated when a contact's composite health score crosses a configurable threshold (suggested default: −15% delta vs. 30-day rolling average). The score should be computed continuously, not batch-scheduled.

2. **Baseline initialisation**: Cards should enter `grey` (insufficient data) status for the first 14 days of contact tracking. The `confidence_level` field should reflect data window size explicitly.

3. **Tone model deployment**: The ACL 2018 feature set (lexical + syntactic + affect features using ANEW, Emolex, PERMA lexicons) is implementable on-device via CoreML or a Python sidecar. The feature set requires no internet access and operates on email body text only. Consider using distilBERT fine-tuned on the Enron corpus for production deployment.

4. **Privacy architecture**: All NLP processing should occur within the macOS app sandbox. Email content should never leave the device. Only aggregated signal values (not raw text) should be written to Supabase. The `signal_timeline` field in the Detail layer should store signal metadata (timestamp, metric, value), not email excerpts.

5. **Feedback loop**: The CEO's card interactions (confirm/dismiss/annotate) should feed back into the baseline calibration model. Dismissed interpretations should lower the prior probability of that interpretation class for that contact in future cards.

6. **Cross-contact synthesis**: At weekly cadence, generate a meta-card summarising relationship health trends across all 15–20 contacts. Use cross-contact patterns to distinguish systemic (CEO's own behaviour change) from bilateral (specific contact cooling) causes.

## Evidence Gaps

The following claims have face validity or theoretical support but lack direct peer-reviewed empirical validation in professional relationship contexts:

- **Topic divergence as a standalone predictor**: LDA/biterm topic modeling is validated for email clustering but no study isolates topic drift as a causal predictor of professional relationship deterioration.[^28]
- **Initiated-vs-received ratio thresholds**: The ONA productivity correlation is established, but no study establishes specific ratio thresholds (e.g., >70% CEO-initiated = concerning) for executive relationships specifically.[^27]
- **Optimal health score weights for non-sales contexts**: All commercial weighting models (Gainsight, Affinity) are calibrated for sales/CS relationships, not executive peer relationships. Weight recalibration based on CEO feedback data is essential.[^14][^5]
- **Reading time norms for C-suite card consumption**: The 15–30 second target is derived from PDB format precedents and attention research, not from a study of executives reading relationship intelligence cards specifically.[^43][^34]
