## DECISIONS

### Category Creation Strategy
- **Anti-category framing is mandatory.** Salesforce's "No Software" campaign succeeded by defining itself against the existing category, not within it. Timed must never position as "AI productivity tool" or "executive assistant." It is a cognitive intelligence system — a category that does not yet exist.
- **Name the category before anyone else does.** Lochhead's Category Pirates framework: the company that names the category wins it. Timed should own "Executive Cognitive Intelligence" as the category name, defined on Timed's terms.
- **Three-devices framing (iPhone model):** Position Timed as three things that already have value independently — observation engine + compounding cognitive model + intelligence delivery — then reveal they are one system. This is how new categories bypass "I already have something for that."
- **Enemy is the status quo, not competitors.** The enemy is scattered attention, reactive calendars, and the "I already have people for that" mindset. Category creation requires an enemy that is a behaviour, not a product.

### Pricing Architecture
- **Individual executive: $24,000-$36,000/year** (personal expense, no procurement friction).
- **Board-mandated cohort: $48,000-$72,000/year** (corporate governance expense, sold as cognitive risk mitigation).
- **No free tier. No trial. No money-back guarantee.** Each signals low confidence. Executive coaching ($500-1000/hr) and management consulting ($5,000-10,000/day) are the reference prices — Timed must anchor against professional services, not software.
- **Veblen goods psychology:** At this buyer level, higher price signals higher quality. Price below $20K/year and it gets filed as "another SaaS tool." Price above $20K and it enters the category of "serious professional investment."
- **Annual commitment only.** Monthly billing signals optionality, which signals the product is disposable. Annual says "this is a commitment to a new way of operating."

### First 100 Users Strategy
- **Selection criteria:** (1) Connectors in C-suite networks (Gladwell's framework), (2) multi-board directors who sit across 3+ company boards (maximum diffusion surface), (3) executives who already invest in executive coaching (proven willingness to invest in self-improvement), (4) leaders in YPO/Vistage peer groups (built-in amplification channel), (5) cognitively curious — executives who read, reflect, journal (highest probability of recognising Timed's value).
- **"Founding Member" language, never "beta tester."** IKEA effect research: when people feel like co-creators, they value the product 63% more and advocate 5x harder. Beta tester = you are testing my unfinished product. Founding member = you are shaping the future of how executives operate.
- **Concierge-first, always.** First 100 get white-glove onboarding — a dedicated relationship, not a product tour. Model on executive coach-client and private banker-client relationship formation. Transition to scaled at user 500+, not before.

### Word-of-Mouth Channels
- **YPO (Young Presidents Organization):** 35,000+ members globally, organised in peer forums of 8-12 CEOs who meet monthly. One placement in a YPO forum = 8-12 high-trust impressions. Target YPO forum chairs first.
- **Board director networks:** Multi-board directors carry tools across companies. One director on 4 boards = 4 potential enterprise deployments. Board-to-board diffusion is the highest-leverage channel.
- **PE/VC portfolio CEO networks:** Private equity firms mandate tools across their portfolio companies. One PE partner who sees value = 15-50 CEO introductions.
- **The dinner conversation test (Berger's STEPPS):** The product must pass the "would an executive mention this at a private dinner" test. Social Currency (using Timed signals cognitive sophistication), Practical Value (the insight was genuinely useful), Stories (the anecdote of the system revealing something the executive didn't know about themselves is inherently narratable).

### The Impressive Moment
- **"The Pattern They Couldn't Name" (ranked #1):** System surfaces a recurring behavioural pattern the executive has never consciously identified — e.g., "You consistently deprioritise strategic planning after board meetings, spending 3x more time on operational triage in the 48hrs post-board." The insight must be true, non-obvious, and immediately recognisable once stated.
- **Engineering target: >70% probability of triggering the aha moment in week 1.** This requires aggressive passive observation in the first 5 days plus the reflection engine running at Opus-level quality from day 1.

### Positioning
- **Complement to executive coaching, not replacement.** Executive coaching is $500-1000/hr, 2-4 sessions/month. Timed is the always-on cognitive layer between sessions. Positioning as replacement threatens a $20B industry and its advocates. Positioning as complement recruits coaches as distribution partners.
- **New category, not new product.** Timed is not a better calendar, not a better email client, not a better to-do app. It is "Executive Cognitive Intelligence" — a category that did not exist before Timed defined it.

### Executive Adoption Psychology Framework

**Drivers:**
1. **Competitive edge signalling** — executives adopt tools that signal they operate at a higher level than peers
2. **Identity integration (Belk's extended self theory)** — the tool becomes part of how the executive sees themselves, like the BlackBerry or Moleskine
3. **Cognitive offloading** — genuine reduction in decision fatigue and information processing burden
4. **Peer validation** — "other executives I respect use this" is the strongest adoption driver at C-suite level
5. **Exclusivity signalling** — invitation-only access signals that the executive was selected, not marketed to

**Resistance Patterns:**
1. "I already have people for that" → Pre-empt: Timed sees patterns your people can't — it observes YOU, not your org
2. "I don't have time to learn new tools" → Pre-empt: zero learning curve, fully passive, no interface to learn
3. "AI can't understand my context" → Pre-empt: it doesn't try to on day 1; it compounds over months; month 6 is the proof
4. "Privacy — I don't want software watching me" → Pre-empt: on-device only, never leaves your machine, you own the model
5. "I've seen AI hype before" → Pre-empt: don't demo features, demo an actual insight about THEM during onboarding
6. "This is too expensive" → Pre-empt: this objection won't come at the target buyer level — if it does, wrong buyer

## ALGORITHMS

### Trust-Building Sequence for Executive Technology Adoption
1. **Week 0 (Pre-install):** Personal introduction from someone the executive trusts (peer referral, not cold outreach). Share nothing about features — share one anonymised insight example that demonstrates depth.
2. **Week 1 (Silent observation):** System installs, observes, says nothing. Executive must NOT feel watched. Zero notifications, zero prompts. Build data foundation.
3. **Week 2 (First insight):** Deliver ONE high-quality insight. Not a summary. Not a dashboard. One sentence that makes the executive stop and think. Quality over quantity — one brilliant insight > ten adequate ones.
4. **Week 3-4 (Calibration):** Ask: "Was that accurate?" Incorporate feedback. Deliver 2-3 insights per week, increasing in depth. The system visibly learns from their response.
5. **Month 2+ (Compounding trust):** Insights reference patterns across weeks. The model demonstrates it remembers and connects. This is when identity integration begins — the executive starts to feel the system "knows" them.

### Concierge-to-Scale Transition Mechanics
- **Users 1-100:** Full concierge. Dedicated relationship manager per 10-15 executives. Weekly personal check-ins. Every insight reviewed by human before delivery.
- **Users 100-500:** Semi-concierge. Relationship manager per 25-30 executives. Bi-weekly check-ins. Automated insight delivery with human QA on flagged items.
- **Users 500-2000:** Scaled concierge. Relationship manager per 50-75 executives. Monthly check-ins. Automated delivery, human escalation only. Community (founding member network) replaces some 1:1 relationship needs.
- **Users 2000+:** Product-led with premium support. Self-serve onboarding (refined from 2000 manual onboardings). Dedicated support for cohort/enterprise accounts only.
- **Critical rule:** Never remove the human layer entirely. At every scale, the executive must feel they can reach a human who knows their account. Bloomberg Terminal retains dedicated support at $24K/seat even at 325,000+ terminals.

### Feedback Loop Without Feature Creep (Von Hippel Lead User Method)
1. **Separate insight feedback from feature requests.** Ask: "Was this insight accurate? Was it useful? What would have made it more useful?" Never ask: "What features do you want?"
2. **Classify feedback into three buckets:** (a) Insight quality — the reflection engine needs tuning, (b) Delivery timing/format — the surface needs work, (c) Feature request — log but do NOT act unless 30%+ of cohort independently surfaces the same need.
3. **Monthly synthesis, not reactive sprints.** Aggregate feedback monthly. Identify patterns. Only act on patterns, never individual requests.
4. **The vision filter:** Every piece of feedback passes through: "Does this make the cognitive model smarter, or does this add a feature?" Only the former ships.

### Engineering Serendipity (Maximising Week-1 Aha Probability)
1. **Over-collect in week 1.** Run all passive signals at maximum sensitivity: email metadata frequency analysis, calendar pattern extraction, application switching behaviour, keystroke rhythm patterns. More data = higher probability of finding a non-obvious pattern.
2. **Run Opus reflection at maximum effort on day 5.** Don't wait for a month of data. Five days of aggressive observation gives enough for Opus to find one pattern worth surfacing.
3. **Pre-compute 5 candidate insights, surface the strongest.** Generate multiple, rank by: (a) non-obviousness, (b) personal relevance, (c) immediate recognisability. Only deliver the highest-ranked.
4. **Timing: deliver the first insight at 7:15 AM on day 6.** Morning, before the executive enters reactive mode. The insight must be waiting when they open their laptop. Not pushed as a notification — present in the morning session.
5. **Fallback: if no non-obvious pattern found, deliver a precise observation.** "You spent 47% of last week in meetings with your direct reports but 0% with their direct reports" is not an aha, but it's concrete enough to demonstrate the system is paying attention.

## APIS & FRAMEWORKS

### Category Pirates / Lochhead Framework
- **Category Design (not product design):** The company that defines and names the category wins 76% of the category's total market cap. Timed must design the category "Executive Cognitive Intelligence" before building features.
- **Lightning Strike:** A concentrated, dramatic moment that announces the category exists. Salesforce's "No Software" protest. For Timed: publish a manifesto on why executives are operating with 19th-century self-awareness in a 21st-century complexity environment.
- **Point of View (POV):** Category creators don't have marketing messages — they have a worldview. Timed's POV: "Every executive operates with a cognitive blind spot that grows with seniority. The higher you rise, the less honest feedback you get about how you actually operate. Timed is the first system that shows you what no one else will tell you."

### Christensen "Competing Against Luck" (Jobs-to-Be-Done)
- **The job:** "Help me understand how I actually operate, not how I think I operate."
- **Hiring criteria:** Executives will "hire" Timed when their current solutions (executive coaching, chief of staff, self-reflection) fail to give them continuous, data-grounded self-awareness.
- **Non-consumption:** The biggest market is executives who currently have NO solution for cognitive self-awareness — not those switching from a competitor.

### Blue Ocean Strategy (Kim & Mauborgne)
- **Eliminate:** Dashboards, metrics, productivity scores, task management, any feature that exists in current executive tools.
- **Reduce:** UI surface area (minimal, invisible), setup complexity (fully passive), notification frequency (almost zero).
- **Raise:** Intelligence depth (Opus-level reflection), personalisation (compounding model), privacy (on-device only).
- **Create:** Cognitive self-awareness as a product category. Compounding intelligence that gets smarter monthly. The "it knows me" experience.

### Berger's STEPPS Framework (Applied to Executive Shareability)
- **Social Currency:** Using Timed signals cognitive sophistication — "I have a system that analyses how I think." Executives share things that make them look thoughtful, not things that make them look like they need help.
- **Triggers:** Morning routine = daily trigger. Every time the executive opens their laptop in the morning, Timed is there. Daily exposure = daily potential share trigger.
- **Emotion:** Awe (the system saw something I didn't) > surprise (how did it know that?) > validation (I suspected that about myself, now I have data).
- **Public:** Timed is private by design — it cannot be public. But the EFFECTS are public: the executive makes better decisions, notices patterns others miss, operates with visible cognitive clarity. The product is invisible; its impact is visible.
- **Practical Value:** Every insight must be actionable enough that the executive can do something different THIS WEEK. Not strategic — tactical and immediate.
- **Stories:** "My cognitive intelligence system told me I avoid strategic planning after board meetings — and it was right." That is a complete, shareable story with a beginning (I have this system), middle (it told me something), and end (it was right).

### Extended Self Theory (Belk)
- **Digital possessions as identity:** Belk's research shows people incorporate their tools into their self-concept. The BlackBerry wasn't a phone — it was part of how executives defined their professional identity.
- **For Timed:** The compounding cognitive model IS the executive's extended self — it is a digital representation of how they think and operate. The longer they use it, the more it becomes "theirs" in a psychological ownership sense. This is the deepest possible switching cost: leaving Timed means leaving behind a model of yourself.
- **Design implication:** The cognitive model must feel like it BELONGS to the executive. Language: "your model," "your patterns," "your cognitive signature." Never "our analysis" or "Timed's insights."

## NUMBERS

- **YPO network:** 35,000+ members in 142 countries, organised in forums of 8-12. Penetrating 1 forum = 8-12 impressions. Penetrating 10 forums = 80-120 impressions. Target: 50 YPO members in first 100 users.

- **PE portfolio leverage:** Average mid-market PE firm has 15-25 portfolio companies. One PE partner adoption = introduction to 15-25 CEOs.

## ANTI-PATTERNS

### Traditional Marketing Destroys Credibility
- **Ads, SEO, content marketing, social media campaigns:** Research on ultra-premium buyer psychology shows these actively DAMAGE perceived value. If executives see a LinkedIn ad for Timed, it is immediately categorised as "another SaaS product." Bloomberg has never run a consumer ad. McKinsey does not do SEO for client acquisition. The channel IS the message.
- **PR/media coverage before product-market fit:** Premature press creates expectations the product cannot yet meet. Coverage should be earned AFTER the first 100 users are advocates, not before.

### Free Tiers Signal Low Value
- **Any free tier, freemium model, or extended trial period** signals that the product is not confident enough to charge from day 1. At the ultra-premium level, free = worthless. Executive coaching does not offer free sessions. Private banking does not offer trial accounts.
- **Exception:** A time-limited "founding member" rate (lower but never free) is acceptable because it signals exclusivity, not low value.

### "Beta Testing" Language
- **Never use "beta," "early access," "testing," or "MVP" with this buyer profile.** These words signal unfinished product and transfer risk to the user. Executives do not volunteer to test unfinished products.
- **Replace with:** "Founding member," "inaugural cohort," "by invitation." The product is not being tested — it is being introduced to its first community.

### Feature Creep from Early Users
- **Executive users will request features that make Timed more like tools they already know** (dashboards, reports, integrations, automations). These requests, if followed, will pull Timed out of its new category and into an existing one.
- **The rule:** Log all feedback. Act only on feedback about insight QUALITY (was it accurate, was it useful, was it timely). Ignore feedback about features unless 30%+ of the cohort independently surfaces the same need.
- **Von Hippel's lead user method:** The most valuable feedback from lead users is about PROBLEMS, not solutions. Ask "what frustrated you?" not "what feature do you want?"

### Failed Category Creation Case Studies
- **Segway:** Tried to create "personal transportation" category. Failed because the problem (walking is slow) wasn't painful enough to justify a $5,000 product that made you look foolish. **Lesson for Timed:** Cognitive overload in executives MUST be validated as genuine, acute pain — not a nice-to-solve.
- **Google Glass:** Tried to create "wearable computing" for consumers. Failed because it was too visible (social stigma), solved no urgent problem, and was positioned as consumer when its value was enterprise. **Lesson for Timed:** Invisible by design. Private by design. Never performative. The executive's use of Timed should be invisible to everyone except them.
- **Google Wave:** Tried to create "real-time collaboration" category. Failed because it merged too many concepts (email + IM + wiki + collaboration), had no single aha moment, and couldn't be explained in one sentence. **Lesson for Timed:** One sentence explanation, one aha moment, one core value proposition. "It watches how you work and tells you what you're not seeing."
- **Common failure pattern across all three:** They announced a new category without first proving the category's value to a small, high-trust group. They went broad before going deep. Timed must go impossibly deep with 100 people before going one person wider.
